About UWMCC

Management Consulting is the industry and practice of helping organizations improve their performance, primarily through the analysis of existing business problems and development of plans for improvement.

The University of Waterloo Management Consulting Club’s (UWMCC) primary focus is to make the University of Waterloo an active recruiting ground for Management Consulting firms. We believe that Waterloo students possess the talent and drive to succeed in the consulting industry; however, most students are either unaware of or lack the required knowledge to pursue these opportunities. We welcome you to grow alongside with club.

We are a registered student club with the Federation of Students at the University of Waterloo.

The UW Management Consulting Club seeks to attract students and help them acquire the basic skills needed to succeed in pursuing opportunities in the consulting industry. This club holds events that not only facilitate the networking process between UW students and recruiters at the targeted consulting firms, but also showcase the talent and motivation of UW students in front of the consulting firms.

Our Services

Résumé Critiquing

IBM professionals review consulting-geared resumes as their way of guiding members in formulating their essential printed representative. The club has provided on their website an outline and some tips that will direct students in drafting one. This service is highly beneficial because the resume often determines your chance for further assessment.

CEO Challenge

This is a two-hour event where representatives from top tier consulting firms, such as McKinsey & Company, Deloitte Consulting and Oliver Wyman, will share their perspective on the industry. It is the best time for students to raise questions, ask for advice on how to break into the field and interact with them in a casual setup.

Case Competitions

Students can enrich their capability to resolve business problems through case competitions. Teams of 3 or 4 shall compete with the other groups. Students can also sign up individually to be matched with other interested participants. The competition is judged by professionals from global management consulting firms.
